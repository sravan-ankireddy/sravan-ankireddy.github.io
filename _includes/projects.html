About me

I am a second year Ph.D student in the department of ECE at the University of Texas at Austin, advised by Prof. Hyeji Kim . I am broadly interested in Information Theory, Machine Learning and Wireless Communications. Presently I am working on augmentation of physical layer communication algorithms using neural networks. I am also interested in exploring the connections between the above fields to gain more insights into the performance of neural algorithms and establish some theoretical gaurantees. Finally, I see myself working at the intersection of theory and systems, to develop novel solutions to some classical problems using the tools of Machine Learning .

In the past, I spent two wonderful years a Research Engineer at Qualcomm working in the Physical Layer Modelling team in Bangalore, India. Previously, I graduated with Bachelors and Masters in Electrical Engineering from IIT Madras in 2019. I have been extremely fortunate to have worked with Dr. Andrew Thangaraj and Dr. Radha Krishna Ganti for my Master's Thesis on efficient encoding and decoding of Polar Codes.

In my spare time I enjoy reading books, watching documentaries and recently developed a love for hiking and backpacking!
